Section 01

Executive Summary

Key findings for Tomek & Karolina
🔴 Centrum Psychiatrii ANIMA is the most serious direct competitive threat to WCP. This is not a distant competitor — ANIMA is a structurally superior organization that is actively expanding into WCP's key markets with advantages WCP cannot easily replicate.

The threat is defined by three critical asymmetries:

1

NFZ Access

ANIMA treats patients reimbursed by NFZ. WCP does not have this option. This gives ANIMA access to a patient pool estimated to be 5–10× larger than the private market in which WCP operates.

2

In-House Psychiatrist

ANIMA has a psychiatrist from 300 PLN. WCP has no psychiatrist, forcing patients to seek pharmacotherapy elsewhere — this creates friction and leakage in the care pathway.

3

Geographic Scale

ANIMA operates from 6 locations (including a new branch in Iława opened in March 2026) vs WCP's 3 locations. They are physically present in more population centres in the region and are actively expanding.

🟡 Bottom Line: ANIMA is not merely competing with WCP for the same patients — ANIMA is competing on a field WCP does not even play on (NFZ), offering a service WCP does not have (psychiatry), from more locations than WCP. The threat is real, growing, and structural.

4.2 In-House Psychiatrist

🔴 ANIMA — Integrated Pathway

Patient → Psychiatrist → Psychotherapist (one roof)

No leakage. Full continuity of care. Pharmacotherapy available without leaving the facility.

🟢 WCP — Fragmented Pathway

Patient → External psychiatrist → WCP psychotherapist

Leakage at every handoff. Increased friction. Risk of losing the patient along the way.

4.3 Six Locations vs Three

  • Two Olsztyn locations vs one WCP
  • Small-town capture: Szczytno, Biskupiec, Węgorzewo
  • Iława threatens WCP's Ostróda market

4.4 Day Ward + PTP Accreditation + Children's Centre

1

Day Ward

A hospital-level service WCP cannot offer. Full inpatient-equivalent treatment in a day format, reimbursed by NFZ.

2

PTP Accreditation

Institutional credibility. Often required by partner facilities and insurance contracts.

3

Dedicated Children/Youth Centre

26 staff in a single branch dedicated entirely to this. A specialisation WCP does not have.

Section 07

Strategic Recommendations

Eight key counter-strategic actions
1

Hire a Psychiatrist P1

Closes the biggest gap, enables integrated care, qualifies for PTP accreditation, and is a prerequisite for NFZ.

2

Secure an NFZ Contract P2

5–10× patient pool expansion. Timeline: 12–24 months. Start planning now.

3

Double Down on ADHD P2

Flagship service, 50% price advantage, MOXO is unique. This is where WCP can dominate.

4

Expand Online Therapy P3

Geographic equaliser, plays to WCP's brand strength. Target: 15% of revenue in 6 months.

5

Attack the Review Weakness P2

SEO for comparative queries, maximise WCP reviews. Not a negative campaign — demonstrate the difference with facts.

6

Build the Therapy-First Brand P3

Lean into warmth, specialisation, community. This is a strategic advantage, not just aesthetics.

7

Optimise the Care Pathway P2

Formalise psychiatrist partnerships, reduce leakage. A temporary solution until in-house hire.

8

Children/Youth Programme P3

Capture parents willing to pay for shorter wait times than NFZ.
