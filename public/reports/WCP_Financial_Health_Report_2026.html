UoP vs B2B: Cost Comparison

🔴 UoP (Employment Contract)

  • Employer ZUS: ~19.5% of gross
  • Severance obligations
  • Paid leave requirements
  • Sick pay coverage
  • Total employer burden: +30-40%

🟢 B2B (Self-Employed)

  • No employer ZUS
  • No severance obligation
  • No paid leave requirements
  • Contractor handles own taxes
  • Direct cost: ~0% burden
💡 Savings Estimate: The UoP → B2B transition saved WCP approximately 200,000+ PLN annually in direct employer costs. This doesn't reduce therapist income — B2B contractors typically set rates that account for their own tax obligations.

Key Financial Risks

Threats · Mitigations · Risk matrix

Critical Risk Factors

gpp_maybe Revenue Concentration HIGH

WCP revenue is concentrated in individual therapy sessions. Any disruption to core service (regulatory, reputational, competitive) would have outsized impact.

Mitigation: Diversify revenue streams — online therapy (15% target), group therapy, couples expansion, corporate EAP contracts.

trending_up ZUS Cost Growth MEDIUM

ZUS contributions grow with headcount and wage inflation. At 500K+ PLN annually, even 10% increase means 50K+ additional cost.

Mitigation: Maintain B2B preference in hiring. Optimize contractor vs employee mix. Monitor ZUS threshold changes.

account_balance Cash Buffer Adequacy MEDIUM

50.6% margin is healthy, but rapid expansion (Bartąg, online therapy infrastructure) could strain cash if not paced correctly.

Mitigation: Maintain 3-month operating expense reserve. Stage investments — Bartąg only after sustained Q2-Q3 performance.

campaign Marketing Efficiency HIGH

Google Ads ROAS at 0.99× means marketing spend is barely breaking even. This is unsustainable for growth.

Mitigation: Immediate optimization (see Google Ads report). Shift budget to brand campaigns. Test organic/social channels.

Methodology Notes
